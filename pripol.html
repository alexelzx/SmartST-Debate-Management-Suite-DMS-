Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 25, 2026

SmartST DMS ("we", "us", or "our") is fundamentally committed to protecting the privacy, security, and integrity of the data generated by our users. This Comprehensive Privacy Policy delineates the precise methodologies by which we collect, utilize, process, and safeguard information across our debate management ecosystem, in strict accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable European Union directives.

1. Information We Collect

To provide our highly synchronized chronometry and administrative services, we must collect specific categories of data.

1.1. Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Data: For Host accounts, we collect and securely encrypt email addresses and authentication credentials via Firebase Authentication.
  • Participant Profiles: When joining a session, we collect user-declared identifiers, including full names, organizational affiliations (e.g., Democrats), and rhetorical alignments (Pro/Con/Neutral).
  • Session Content: Ephemeral data generated during active discourse, including parole queue requests, moderator-issued warnings, and localized "Debator Notes."

1.2. Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage Data: We automatically record diagnostic metadata regarding how the Services are accessed and used. This may include IP addresses, browser types, latency metrics, and timestamped actions within the application interface.
  • Cookies and Local Storage: We employ HTML5 Local Storage to maintain secure session states (e.g., Moderator PIN verification) and ensure uninterrupted connectivity during critical forum proceedings.

2. How We Use Your Information

We strictly utilize the collected data to execute and enhance the core functionalities of the SmartST DMS. We do not engage in data brokering, nor do we sell personal information to third-party advertising entities.

  • Service Delivery: To broadcast real-time updates across the Participant, Host, and Projector interfaces.
  • Security Operations: To authenticate executive commands, prevent unauthorized session alterations, and enforce moderator directives.
  • System Integrity: To monitor cloud database performance and troubleshoot latency anomalies.

3. Legal Bases for Processing (EU Users)

If you reside within the European Economic Area (EEA), our legal basis for collecting and using the personal information described above depends on the specific context in which we collect it. We process data primarily on the basis of Legitimate Interest (to operate and secure the platform) and Contractual Necessity (to deliver the requested synchronization services).

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Your data is strictly compartmentalized. We only share information under the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We utilize Google Firebase (Firestore and Authentication) to host and process the real-time data streaming necessary for the application.
  • Within the Session: Your declared name and team affiliation will be visible to all participants, the Moderator, and the public Projector view within your specific session code.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities located within the jurisdiction of France or the EU.

5. Data Security

We deploy robust, enterprise-grade security protocols. Real-time data access is governed by strict Firestore Security Rules, ensuring that only authenticated Hosts can generate sessions, and only active session participants can update the parole queue. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data Retention

We adhere to the principle of data minimization. Debate session data, including rosters, chronometry logs, and participant notes, are maintained within the active Firestore database solely for the duration required to execute the forum. Upon session termination by the Host, or periodic system purging, such ephemeral data is slated for permanent deletion.

7. Your Data Protection Rights

Under the GDPR, you possess comprehensive rights regarding your personal data:

  • The right to access: You have the right to request copies of your personal data.
  • The right to rectification: You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate.
  • The right to erasure: You have the right to request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • The right to restrict processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data.
